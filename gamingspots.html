      Luckily, the city have some places where you can take your gaming outside into the real world, where you can use it as a retreat from all the monotony, and where you can use test your gaming skills to impress your significant other (probably not, but maybe).

Here are the best places to play video games in NYC while you socialize.



Barcade
388 Union Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11211

      Barcade® is the Original Arcade Bar that combines a classic bar atmosphere with an amusement arcade. The original Barcade opened in 2004 in a former metal shop on Union Avenue in Williamsburg, Brooklyn and has since expanded into a chain of arcade bars with locations in the northeastern United States.
      The video game selection is mostly from the classic period of the early 1980’s and ranges through the early 1990’s, although there may be titles as early as 1975 and as recent as the mid-2000’s in rotation. Most locations will have between 40 and 60 games on the floor and their is also a pinball selection that includes many current titles as well as classic machines from the 1980’s and 1990’s.

Nintendo New York
10 Rockefeller Plaza, New York, NY 10020

      Nintendo New York (previously known as The Pokémon Center and Nintendo World) is the flagship specialty store for the video game corporation Nintendo. The two-story, 10,000-square-foot location was formerly called The Pokémon Center, which opened on November 16, 2001. It ran until January 2005, when it closed for remodeling and reopening as Nintendo World four months later. On January 19, 2016, the Nintendo World was closed for renovation and re-opened its doors on February 19, 2016, and was renamed Nintendo New York. The store sells a wide variety of Nintendo related video games and merchandise, including apparel, hardware, and accessories that are exclusive to the store.
      On the second level of the store, there are kiosks with various Nintendo Switch games running and Nintendo 3DS stations available, allowing anyone to play. The store regularly holds tournaments and shows for new games, giving early releases and prizes to winners and also held screenings for multiple official Nintendo broadcasts. On that same floor, it also serves as a museum featuring past Nintendo game systems and peripherals, like the Nintendo Power Glove, original Nintendo Entertainment System, and the Nintendo Famicom from Japan.

VR World NYC
4 E 34th St, New York, NY 10016

      Opened on June 2017, located in the heart of Midtown Manhattan and just steps from the Empire State Building, VR World is acclaimed as the largest Virtual Reality entertainment destination in North America and the Western Hemisphere. An attraction by day and an entertainment lounge by night, the location has three floors of the most diverse virtual reality experiences that can be used in a public setting. Choose from over fifty virtual reality experiences including 360 degree art movies, music videos, multi-player games and captivating simulations.
      Visitors will wear either an Oculus, HTC Vive, or Samsung Gear headset with each having a set of different experiences to explore. Some experiences are completely hands-free and some require use of controls similar to electronic games. With their revolutionary approach to turning an emerging media into a socially engaging experience, VR World is redefining entertainment by merging culture with cutting-edge technology and turning immersive experiences into lasting memories.

Action Burger
292 Graham Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11211

      A pop-culture hub, Action Burger merges sci-fi, comics, and video games into a fun restaurant locale. On July 2017, it was closed for renovation and re-opened its doors on October 2017, having been upgraded to "Action Burger 2.0." The location is now twice the size it used to be and has added a new sci-fi/sports bar dubbed Planet Xenos.
      Their menu consists of favorites such as the Mac & Cheese Bacon Burger, as well as burgers named after characters from the restaurant's own comics series, served in a space filled with comics and arcade games. While there, guests can choose from a vast amount of classic NES, Xbox, and Gamecube games to play right at the table along with tons of other arcade and console games found throughout the restaurant.

Plug and Play Gaming Café
150-07 Northern Blvd, Flushing, NY 11354

      This Flushing, Queens gaming café offers a number of popular games on its superfast computers and 27-inch monitors. Plug and Play’s games include League of Legends, Overwatch, World of Warcraft, and Counter Strike. The store opened in late 2016 but is already popular among local gamers.

Two Bit’s Retro Arcade
153 Essex St, New York, NY 10002

      Located on the Lower East Side of Manhattan, Two Bit’s Retro Arcade features 20 classic arcade games from the 1980s and 90s. Visitors can play games like Frogger, Pacman and Tetris while drinking craft beers, wines, and sake. Food such as hot dogs and pizza can also be bought at the bar, and movies are projected onto the back wall.

Chinatown Fair Family Fun Center
8 Mott St, New York, NY 10038

      Chinatown Fair Family Fun Center (previously known as Chinatown Fair) is a video arcade center located in Chinatown, Manhattan and historically catered toward competitive fighting games.
      The original arcade opened in 1944 and in 1991 had switched focus to competitive fighting games. During its time, it had been featured in The Devils Advocate with Al Pacino and Falling in Love with Meryl Streep and Robert DeNiro. In 2010, The New York Times wrote that Chinatown Fair was among the "Last Great Arcade" in the city.
      When it closed in February 2011, competitive fighting game players relocated to Next Level, a Brooklyn arcade, which opened on that same year. But the Chinatown Fair reopened in May 2012 as Chinatown Fair Family Fun Center and under different management. The arcade was the focus of a 2015 documentary called "The Lost Arcade" which was released on August 12, 2016.

Next Level
874 4th Ave, Brooklyn, New York 11232

      A video arcade center located in Brooklyn, New York, Next Level is considered a successor to Chinatown Fair and the new "premier hub" of the United States competitive fighting game scene. Weekly tournaments at the arcade are live streamed.

Waypoint Cafe NYC
65 Ludlow St, New York, NY 10002

      Welcome to video game nerd heaven at Waypoint Cafe NYC. When you enter, you can grab a coffee up front and settle in with your laptop, or you can lay down a few bucks and go back into the perfectly lit hall of desktop computers and ergonomically designed gamer chairs and play practically any popular online PC game. With extremely reasonable prices, and the most competitive online games available, Waypoint is the perfect place to brush up on your skills.
      If you are good on your game, you can sign up for one of the many tournaments Waypoint hosts. In fact, the New York esports’ Overwatch team competes at Waypoint and just happens to be sponsored by the owner of the New York Mets. Serious video gaming happens here, and the cafe’s internet connection is fast enough to handle it.
